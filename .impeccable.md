# Used Car Inspection Guide — Ebook Sales Site

## Design Context

### Users
People about to spend real money on a used car who are afraid of being ripped off.
Primarily anxious first-time buyers and casual bargain hunters browsing on a phone or
laptop, often the evening before going to look at a car. The job: feel confident they
can spot a lemon and walk away from a bad deal. Conversion goal: buy a $6.99 PDF guide.

### Brand Personality
Protective, sharp, quietly expert. The voice of someone who has crawled under a hundred
cars and will not let you get scammed. Confident, plain-spoken, zero fluff. Three words:
**protective, expert, no-nonsense**. Emotional goal: turn anxiety into a sense of control.

### Aesthetic Direction
Three sibling variations explore the same story through different worlds:
- **Cinematic Showroom** — dark, premium automotive launch. High-contrast serif display,
  scroll-driven 3D car walkaround, dramatic light, slow weighty motion.
- **Garage Blueprint** — industrial cyanotype. Schematic grid, wireframe 3D car,
  technical mono labels, inspection-checkpoint markers.
- **Trustworthy Editorial** — bright warm magazine / consumer-report. Slab headlines,
  serif body, generous whitespace, calm credibility, light theme.
Anti-references: generic SaaS landing pages, glassmorphism, purple→blue gradients,
neon-on-dark, stock-photo hero with centered text.

### Design Principles
1. **The car is the hero.** A real 3D car (or its schematic) anchors every variation;
   the story is a guided walkaround, not a feature list.
2. **Story over sections.** Scroll = the inspection journey: arrive → walk around →
   under the hood → test drive → close the deal. Parallax conveys depth, not decoration.
3. **Earned trust, honestly.** No fake authority. Trust comes from a real sample-checklist
   preview and concrete specifics, not invented credentials.
4. **Self-contained & robust.** No external image dependencies — SVG + Three.js + CSS art
   so it always looks intentional, even offline. Photos can be swapped in later.
5. **Restraint in motion.** One well-orchestrated scroll narrative beats scattered
   micro-animations. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
